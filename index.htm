Chapter One

The beginning

In the beginning there was CADIVI.

For many Venezuelans, it is a fact that we have to go through a very long process to come to Ireland and study English. It could take more time preparing your journey than living in Dublin.

The adventure for us began many months, sometimes years, before leaving Venezuela. In my case it started in August 2013 when I found a blog post about studying English in Ireland.

The first step buy a plane ticket, and it means 'choosing a date'. A dangerous step because you will be rely on many steps later that not depend on you and can result in losing your money.

The second is about papers: letters from the college, embassy, stamps, more papers, residence letter, and many more documents. In my case my CADIVI folder had 57 pages, more than this but I am afraid.

The third step is the most risky. Because you may have to lock, which is in some cases your life savings, the money that cover the cost of your travel for 1 or 2 months until you get the CADIVI veredict.

In my case I got the approval in January, 2 and a half weeks before the ticket day.

After that, there were a lot of saying good bye meetings with friends and family. Very nice moments.

Chapter Three

Venezuela

Currently, Venezuelan people are living in difficult times, we are protesting against the poor living quality, the lack of security in our cities and many other things that are not going well at this moment.

It is hard to be here and see how people die just trying to get better live conditions in dangerous protests every day.

My Venezuelan partners and I are on the same boat, we left the country to study, improve and get better live conditions.

I never forget my country, this current situation gives me the strength to be better person and prepare myself for the future and help raising my country after this storm finish.
